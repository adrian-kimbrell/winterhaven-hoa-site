From the Commons

A Season of Monsoon Skies & Open Gates

There is a particular hush that settles over Winterhaven Village in early June, when the palo verde throws its lace-thin shade across the lanes and neighbors drift out at dusk to watch the Catalinas turn from gold to rose. This week the calendar fills: the pool reopens, a pollinator garden takes root, and a sunset potluck looms on the horizon.

This Week at Winterhaven Village

Bulletin
Amenities

Pool Resurfacing Begins Monday

Crews arrive before the heat sets in Monday to begin the long-anticipated resurfacing of the community pool. The deck will be closed through the work, with the basin drained and a fresh plaster finish to follow — the aim is to have residents swimming laps under the Catalinas by July. Please keep little ones and curious corgis clear of the fencing.

— Facilities Committee
Grounds

New Native Pollinator Garden at the East Gate

A bed of Sonoran natives now greets visitors at the east gate: penstemon, fairy duster, and desert marigold are in, planted to draw hummingbirds, native bees, and the occasional dazzled passerby. The planting asks almost nothing of the irrigation line and honors the desert it grows from. Volunteers are welcome on cooler mornings to help it settle in.

— Landscape Volunteers
Events

Sunset Potluck on the Commons — June 21

Mark your calendars: the Sunset Potluck returns to the commons on the longest day of the year. Bring a dish to share and a lawn chair to sit a spell as the light goes long over the foothills. A sign-up sheet circulates this week so we don't end up, as in years past, with eleven varieties of pasta salad and no dessert.

— Social Committee

CC&R Corner — Question of the Week

“Can I park my RV in my driveway overnight?”

In short: Yes — for up to 72 hours for the purpose of loading or unloading. Beyond that window, recreational vehicles must be stored off-street. See Section 4.3.
