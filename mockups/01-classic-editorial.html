From the Hilltop

A Season of Long Evenings & Open Gates

There is a particular quiet that settles over Winterhaven Village in early June, when the pines throw their long shadows across the lanes and neighbors drift out to porches with the last of the daylight. This week the calendar fills: the pool reopens, a garden takes root, and a potluck looms on the horizon.

This Week at Winterhaven Village

Bulletin
Amenities

Pool Resurfacing Begins Monday

Crews arrive at first light Monday to begin the long-anticipated resurfacing of the community pool. The deck will be closed through the work, weather permitting, with the basin drained and a fresh plaster finish to follow. Residents are asked to keep little ones and curious corgis clear of the fencing.

— Facilities Committee
Grounds

New Native Plant Garden at the North Entrance

A bed of drought-tolerant natives — manzanita, sage, and ceanothus among them — has been planted to greet visitors at the north entrance. The planting honors the hillside's own chaparral and asks nothing of the sprinklers. Volunteers are welcome Saturday mornings to help it settle in.

— Landscape Volunteers
Events

Summer Potluck — June 21, Save the Date

Mark your calendars: the annual Summer Potluck returns to the clubhouse lawn on the longest day of the year. Bring a dish to share and a chair to sit a spell. A sign-up sheet circulates this week so we don't end up, as in years past, with eleven varieties of potato salad and no dessert.

— Social Committee

CC&R Corner — Question of the Week

“Can I park my RV in my driveway overnight?”

In short: Yes — for up to 72 hours for the purpose of loading or unloading. Beyond that window, recreational vehicles must be stored off-street. See Section 4.3.
